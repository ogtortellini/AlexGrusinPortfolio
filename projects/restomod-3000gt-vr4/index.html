My Restomod 3000GT VR4 Project

This project chronicles my ongoing technical and educational journey in restoring and modifying a 1997 Mitsubishi 3000GT VR4. Spanning multiple years, I've taken this car from a rusted, non-functional platform to a high-performance, street-capable machine. Since starting in 2018, the build has involved a full chassis and subframe restoration, two engine rebuilds, a transmission overhaul, and a highly iterative ECU tuning process. For me, this project serves as a hands-on laboratory for learning systems integration, reliability engineering, and motorsport-grade calibration.

1. Introduction & My Early Restoration (2018)

In June 2018, at age 14, I began this project while visiting family. My initial repairs took place at my uncle's house, where my father and I fixed a leaking rear brake line and restored the AC functionality. Despite my limited experience at the time, I absorbed foundational mechanical skills during these early repairs.

The 3000GT VR4 as it was first found.

Addressing a critical brake line leak early on.

Afterward, my father and I drove the car 600 miles home. It was in severe disrepair: no air conditioning, holes through the floor, and only a temporary brake line fix. The images below show some of the rust I discovered upon closer inspection.

Significant rust damage found in the passenger floor.

Corrosion in the rear fender well.

The rear subframe showed extensive rust.

That summer, I initiated a full teardown of the undercarriage. I cut out severely rusted floor sections and replaced them with junkyard donor panels, which I then welded in. I removed, stripped, refinished, and reassembled both front and rear subframes with polyurethane bushings, new axles, fresh seals, and rubber lines. This involved removing the old bushings, prepping and painting the subframe, and then reassembling everything.

I also rebuilt all four brake calipers and installed new e-brake shoes inside the rear rotors. The undercarriage was soda blasted to remove rust, then coated for protection. Many old parts were discarded, and I sourced replacements from donor cars, even for small items like body panel clips.

During this first year, I only performed basic engine maintenance (seals, timing belt, water pump), leaving the engine internals untouched.

My Challenges & Learnings:

  • I gained foundational mechanical skills through hands-on repair of critical systems like brakes and AC.
  • I learned rust remediation techniques, including cutting, welding, and panel replacement.
  • I experienced the importance of systematic disassembly and reassembly, especially with suspension components.
  • I came to understand the extent of neglect and its impact on vehicle safety and functionality.

2. Daily Driving & Mastering Manual (2019)

By September 2019, the car was roadworthy. I taught myself to drive manual in it and began using the 3000GT as my daily driver. The vehicle remained mechanically stable through mid-2020, during which time I gained valuable real-world experience.

My Challenges & Learnings:

  • I mastered driving a manual transmission in a car I had substantially rebuilt myself.
  • I gained real-world experience in vehicle operation and identifying early signs of mechanical issues.
  • Through daily use, I built confidence in the reliability of my restoration work.

3. My Transmission Rebuild (2020)

By fall 2020, driveability issues led me to initiate a full teardown of the W6MG1 6-speed manual transmission. Symptoms included grinding into 3rd gear, difficulty holding reverse, and hard engagement of 1st. The teardown revealed severely worn synchro friction surfaces on 3rd and reverse, a broken roll pin on the 3/4 shift fork, and wear on 1st/2nd gear teeth and sliders.

The transmission case being prepped and painted during the rebuild.

Interior disassembly was necessary for accessing transmission linkages and other components.

My rebuild included new 3/4 and reverse hub/slider assemblies, replacement of input and intermediate shaft bearings, new seals, shift detents, stabilizer bushings, and refreshed linkage and bushings to reduce slop. I completed the reassembly by late 2020.

My Challenges & Learnings:

  • This was my first full experience with gearbox internals, allowing me to understand precision engagement geometry and wear behavior.
  • I learned to diagnose transmission issues based on symptoms and internal inspection.
  • I made decisions on component replacement vs. refurbishment (e.g., I considered carbon synchros vs. OEM due to cost/performance tradeoffs, ultimately not using them).
  • I developed the meticulousness required for assembling complex mechanical systems like a transmission.

4. First Engine Failure & My OEM Rebuild (Nov 2020 – Sept 2021)

In November 2020, the factory engine, which I had not internally rebuilt up to that point, failed catastrophically. The images below show the initial teardown.

Beginning the engine teardown process after the failure.

The engine bay, empty, awaiting the rebuilt engine.

The original engine disassembled down to the bare block.

Between November 2020 and April 2021, my dad and I carefully evaluated options. We considered a forged performance rebuild featuring Ross pistons and high-compression gaskets, but ultimately I opted for an OEM-spec configuration due to cost, complexity, and my intended use for the car. My rebuild included a cleaned and honed block (by MMW), a polished OEM crankshaft, stock pistons and rods, a full OEM gasket kit including MLS-style Athena head gaskets, refreshed oil pump, water pump, and timing components. I verified ring gaps and bearing clearances using plastigage and micrometers and carefully torqued and staged ARP main studs. Sourcing good cylinder heads was a challenge, but I found a suitable set from a donor car.

Sourcing cylinder heads from a donor car.

The assembly process involved careful preparation of all components, including painting the block and installing new bearings, gaskets, and pumps. Upgraded parts like titanium valve springs and retainers were also incorporated.

The final assembly stages included installing the refreshed heads, powdercoated valve covers and intake manifold, new fuel rails, new engine mounts, and new turbos.

My Challenges & Learnings:

  • Navigating the decision-making process for a major engine rebuild, balancing performance goals with budget and practicality.
  • Deepening my understanding of engine component selection and the implications of OEM vs. aftermarket parts.
  • Mastering precision measurement techniques (plastigage, micrometers) for critical engine tolerances.
  • The emotional significance of hearing an engine I rebuilt run for the first time.
  • The importance of methodical assembly and torque sequencing for long-term engine reliability.

5. My ECU Tuning Phase 1 (Jan–Feb 2022)

With the rebuilt engine complete, I began tuning the car using a Chrome ECU, Tactrix OpenPort 2.0 cable, and a wideband O2 sensor. My tuning efforts were supported by Morgan of RacerX Performance. The calibration process involved:

  • Establishing a stable idle and base fuel map.
  • Calibrating for cold start and warm-up enrichment.
  • Initial road tuning to adjust fuel trims and ignition timing under various load conditions.
  • Data logging and iterative refinement of the tune based on sensor feedback.

My Challenges & Learnings:

  • Steep learning curve in understanding ECU logic, fuel maps, and ignition timing.
  • Developing a systematic approach to data logging and analysis.
  • Learning to interpret wideband O2 sensor data to optimize air/fuel ratios.
  • The iterative nature of tuning and the patience required to achieve desired results.

6. My ECU Tuning Phase 2 (Mar–Dec 2023)

This phase focused on refining the tune for drivability and reliability. I worked on:

  • Fine-tuning acceleration enrichment and transient throttle response.
  • Optimizing cruise conditions for fuel economy and smooth operation.
  • Implementing basic boost control strategies.
  • Addressing any knock or sensor irregularities observed during extended driving.

My Challenges & Learnings:

  • Balancing performance with long-term engine health.
  • Understanding the interplay between different ECU parameters.
  • Troubleshooting sensor issues and their impact on tuning.
  • Gaining more confidence in making incremental changes to the tune.

7. My ECU Tuning Phase 3 (Jan 2023 – Feb 2024)

The latest phase involved more advanced tuning, including the integration of new hardware and pushing performance limits safely. Key activities included installing and tuning for larger 720cc injectors, implementing a MAF-less (Speed Density) setup, fine-tuning boost control, extensive data logging under full load, and experimenting with different fuel types.

My Challenges & Learnings:

  • Mastering Speed Density tuning, which requires a deeper understanding of engine volumetric efficiency.
  • Calibrating for larger injectors and managing precise fuel delivery.
  • The complexities of advanced boost control strategies.
  • The critical importance of knock detection and prevention when pushing for higher performance.
  • Continuously learning and adapting as I introduced new variables into the system.

9. Summary & My Current Status (2025)

As of early 2025, my 3000GT VR4 is a testament to years of dedication and learning. It's a fully operational, high-performance vehicle that I regularly enjoy driving. The journey from a neglected shell to its current state has been incredibly rewarding, providing me with invaluable hands-on experience in mechanical engineering, problem-solving, and project management. While any project car is arguably never "finished," the car is now in a reliable and highly enjoyable state. Future plans are modest, focusing on continued maintenance, minor refinements, and perhaps exploring advanced data acquisition for performance analysis. This project has solidified my passion for automotive engineering and has equipped me with a practical skill set that complements my academic studies.
