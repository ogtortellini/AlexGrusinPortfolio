Custom Electric Skateboard

1. Introduction

I started riding an electric skateboard in my freshman year of college. Quickly, my mind was opened to the possibilities, especially as a means of local commuting. To the chagrin of my contemporaries, I became an advocate for the popularization of PEVs. After owning a few electric skateboards, I took what I learned to build a custom electric skateboard with advanced motor control and a long-range battery.

2. Design Considerations

Before I started building, I set a few clear priorities: long range, comfort on rough terrain, and reliability in all weather. Here are the most important factors I considered:

  • Range: I knew I needed a high-capacity battery and efficient power management to avoid frequent recharging.
  • Acceleration & Braking: I wanted motors with enough torque for quick starts and safe, controlled stops in busy campus traffic.
  • Ride Quality: Large wheels were a must for rolling over cracks and rough terrain without losing comfort.
  • Weather Resistance: I planned to seal all electronics and enclosures so I could ride in the rain without worry.
  • Durability: Every part I picked had to be tough enough for year-round, daily use.
  • Weight & Portability: I balanced strong materials with the need to carry the board between classes.
  • Comfort: I shaped the deck and chose grip tape for long rides and secure footing.
  • Looks: I wanted a custom paint job and finish that would make my board stand out on campus.

3. Donor Board: Meepo V4S

I sourced the wheels, hub motors, and trucks from a Meepo V4S electric skateboard. These parts matched my riding style and had already proven themselves reliable, so they made a great foundation for my custom project.

4. Custom Battery Pack: 10s2p P45B Cells

I built the battery pack using a 10s2p configuration with Molicel P45B cells—10 in series for 36V, and 2 in parallel for a total of 20 cells. Each cell had a nominal capacity of 4.5Ah, so my pack delivered 9Ah (324Wh at nominal voltage). This gave me the long-range capability I wanted, letting me commute for days without worrying about recharging.

Theoretical Range Estimation

  • Battery capacity: 324 Wh (9Ah × 36V)
  • Average consumption: ~10-15 Wh per mile (depending on speed, my weight, terrain, and riding style)
  • Conservative estimate (15 Wh/mi): 324 Wh ÷ 15 Wh/mi ≈ 21.6 miles
  • Optimistic estimate (10 Wh/mi): 324 Wh ÷ 10 Wh/mi ≈ 32.4 miles

In real-world use, my range depended on my weight, speed, terrain, wind, and how aggressively I accelerated. For most campus commutes and moderate riding, I got 20–30 miles per charge—much more than typical budget boards, which usually deliver only 8–12 miles.*

*Budget eboard range estimate based on published specs for entry-level models such as the Meepo V4S and WowGo 2S Max. [Meepo V4S Specs]
