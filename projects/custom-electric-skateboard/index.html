Custom Electric Skateboard

Built a custom electric skateboard with advanced motor control and long-range battery.

Design Considerations

The main goal in designing my own electric skateboard was to create a long-range board suitable for commuting around campus without the need for frequent recharging. To achieve this, I prioritized a high-capacity battery and efficient power management. Other important design considerations included:

  • Torquey Motors: Powerful motors were selected to provide strong takeoff acceleration and effective braking, ensuring safety and control in stop-and-go campus environments.
  • Large Wheels: Oversized wheels help the board handle rough terrain, cracks, and uneven surfaces commonly found on outdoor paths.
  • Waterproofing: All electronics and enclosures were sealed to allow safe riding on wet roads and in rainy conditions.
  • Reliability: Every component was chosen and tested for durability, making the board a dependable daily driver for year-round use.
  • Material Selection: The deck and enclosure materials were chosen for optimal strength-to-weight ratio, balancing performance and portability.
  • Ergonomics & Comfort: The deck shape and grip tape were designed for comfortable long rides and secure footing.
  • Visual Appeal: Custom paint and finish give the board a unique look that stands out on campus.

These considerations guided every step of the design and build process, resulting in a board that is not only practical and reliable, but also fun and visually striking.

Donor Board: Meepo V4S

To kickstart the build, I sourced key components from a Meepo V4S electric skateboard. Specifically, I reused the wheels, hub motors, and trucks from this board. These parts were chosen because they fit my ergonomic preferences and riding style, providing a familiar and comfortable feel. The Meepo V4S components are known for their reliability and performance, making them an excellent foundation for my custom build.

Custom Battery Pack: 10s2p P45B Cells

The first major component I designed for this board was a custom battery pack using a 10s2p configuration with Molicel P45B cells. This setup provides 10 cells in series (for a nominal voltage of 36V) and 2 in parallel, resulting in a total of 20 cells. Each P45B cell has a nominal capacity of 4.5Ah, so the pack delivers a total capacity of 9Ah (324Wh at nominal voltage). This high capacity allows for long-range commuting, minimizing the need for frequent recharging and supporting my goal of reliable campus transportation.

Project Overview

This project involved designing and building a custom electric skateboard from the ground up. Key features include a high-efficiency brushless motor, custom battery pack for extended range, and a robust deck with a unique paint job. The project required skills in mechanical design, electronics, and fabrication.

Technical Highlights

  • Custom deck design and paintwork
  • Brushless DC motor with advanced speed controller
  • High-capacity lithium-ion battery pack
  • Laser-cut skateboard rack for storage and display
  • Hands-on fabrication and assembly
